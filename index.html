WHY THE WHEEL OF TIME

An Epic Fantasy with Powerful Women

*The Wheel of Time* TV show brings Robert Jordan’s epic fantasy to life with a rich world and a standout cast, especially its diverse and powerful female characters. From the enigmatic Moiraine to the fierce Nynaeve and ambitious Egwene, women drive the story in ways rarely seen in mainstream fantasy. The show gives them depth, flaws, and agency, making it a refreshing shift from traditional, male-dominated narratives.

While it makes changes to the books, the adaptation skillfully navigates the challenge of condensing such a vast story. Streamlining plots and altering arcs were necessary to fit the medium while preserving the heart of Jordan’s work. Though some changes may surprise longtime fans, they make the show more accessible and engaging, ensuring it stands strong as both an adaptation and a compelling fantasy series in its own right.

LOCATIONS

Some of the main shooting locations

Two Rivers

Where was it shot?

Near the small village of Tetín, tucked into an old quarry, is the exterior set for the Two Rivers.

Why is it important?

The Two Rivers is the heart of The Wheel of Time, a quiet, isolated village that becomes the starting point for the epic journey. Its humble origins contrast with the grand destiny of its characters, emphasizing themes of growth, fate, and the power of ordinary people to shape the world.

Aiel Waste

Where was it shot?

The production spent a significant amount of time filming in various locations around South Africa, for example the Atlantis Dunes.

Why is it important?

The Aiel Waste is a harsh, unforgiving land that shapes the fierce and resilient Aiel people, setting them apart from the rest of the world. It plays a crucial role in the story's exploration of identity, prophecy, and cultural contrast, adding depth to the world and the characters tied to it.

LEARN MORE

Tar Valon

Where was it shot?

Jordan Studios can be found in Prague, which has multiple sound stages as well as production offices, and a backlot that houses the Tar Valon exterior set.

Why is it important?

Tar Valon is the political and magical center of the world, home to the powerful Aes Sedai and the iconic White Tower. As a symbol of knowledge, authority, and mystery, it anchors much of the story's intrigue and power struggles.
